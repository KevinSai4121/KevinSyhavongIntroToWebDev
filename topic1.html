The Birth of the World Wide Web (Web 1.0 Era)

The web began at CERN in 1989–1991 as a system for linking scientific documents using URLs, HTTP, and HTML. Early “Web 1.0” sites were mostly static: organizations published pages, and users read them.

Milestones (1990s)

  • 1991: First public website; early browsers render simple HTML.
  • 1993–1994: Mosaic and Netscape popularize the web.
  • Late 1990s: CSS, JavaScript, and the DOM enable presentation and basic interactivity.

Takeaway

Web 1.0 established the core conventions (URI/URL, HTTP, HTML) and the idea of linking documents across the globe.
