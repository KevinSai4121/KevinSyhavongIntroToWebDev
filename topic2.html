From Social Web to Web 3.0: The Internet Evolves

In the 2000s, “Web 2.0” described a shift toward user-generated content, social platforms, and rich client-side apps. In the 2010s–2020s, “Web 3.0” discussions focused on linked data and decentralized, programmable networks.

Web 2.0 Characteristics

  • Social participation (blogs, wikis, social networks)
  • APIs and mashups; AJAX for asynchronous updates
  • Platform ecosystems and app stores

Web 3.0 Threads

  • Semantic Web & linked data
  • Decentralized identity and data ownership
  • Smart contracts and programmable value

Pros & Cons

  • Pros: Rich experiences, global collaboration, new business models
  • Cons: Centralization risks (2.0), complexity and interoperability challenges (3.0)
